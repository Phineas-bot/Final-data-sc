# Key Insights

1. High activity users are clearly distinguished by both total transaction volume and frequency, making volume a primary segmentation signal.
2. Balance inquiries and account adjustments appear in all segments, but they occur more often among medium and high users, indicating active account monitoring.
3. Failed payment attempts are an important behavioral signal; they are concentrated in higher activity users and can reveal points of friction or retry behavior.
4. Weekend usage is higher for medium and high activity users, suggesting that the most engaged customers rely on mobile money services throughout the week.
5. Payment and transfer transactions are more predictive of activity level than airtime-only usage, which is more common among lower activity users.
6. User activity is best captured by engineered features that combine volume, frequency, and failure rate, rather than any single raw metric.
